Meet Payne and Cali

Two journalism students bond over their hatred of fungus.

By Rachel Lawrence

Calista

Calista (known better as Cali) is a junior journalism major (formerly Bio-E) from Frederick, Maryland . On a Pre-Law track, Cali switched to journalism to foster her love of reading and writing, which she enjoyed much more than STEM.

“I love science, I hate the math,” she said.

Given the ability to possess any superpower, Cali would teleport to get places with ease, or learn to talk to animals, specifically to talk to her boston terrier, Coconut.

Payne

Payne is a sophomore from Baltimore, Maryland. The journalism major found his love of the industry four years ago. After watching sports growing up, something “just clicked” for him during his sophomore year of high school. Payne also has a specific interest in graphic design.

If he could have any superpower, Payne would choose to fly, saying that it would make it easier for him to get from place to place without a car.

Where Payne and Cali agree most is in their avid distaste for mushrooms. Originally, Payne listed his least favorite vegetable as green beans, saying that the texture is what offsets him. However, after Cali mentioned that hers was mushrooms, Payne quickly agreed.

“I can tolerate a green bean,” Payne said. “I can’t tolerate mushrooms.”
